Banksy

Banksy is a mysterious and anonymous England-based street artist, political activist, and film director. His satirical street art and subversive epigrams combine dark humor with graffiti executed in a distinctive stenciling technique. His works often engage with social and political themes.

Yayoi Kusama

Known for her polka dots and infinity mirror rooms, Kusama's art blends obsession, infinity, and personal experience.

Learn More
Yayoi Kusama

Yayoi Kusama is a Japanese contemporary artist who works primarily in sculpture and installation but is also active in painting, performance, and fashion. Known for her obsession with polka dots and mirrors, her art reflects a unique mix of psychological trauma and infinite beauty.

Frida Kahlo

A Mexican artist known for her surreal, vivid self-portraits exploring identity, pain, and human experience.

Learn More
Frida Kahlo

Frida Kahlo was a Mexican painter known for her many portraits and works inspired by the nature and artifacts of Mexico. She used a folk art style to explore themes such as identity, the human body, and death. Her deeply personal works often depict her pain and passion.

Damien Hirst

Famous for his provocative work that challenges ideas of life, death, and beauty. Known for "The Physical Impossibility of Death in the Mind of Someone Living".

Learn More
Damien Hirst

Damien Hirst is a British artist, entrepreneur, and art collector. He is one of the Young British Artists (YBAs) who dominated the art scene in the UK during the 1990s. Hirst is known for artworks that explore themes of death and decay, including preserved animals and medical motifs.
